## Who I Am

I am an undergraduate at Kyung Hee University (Software Convergence, expected graduation 2026) based in Yongin, South Korea. My current work is oriented toward graduate research in robot perception, geometric vision, and deployable manipulation systems.

This site exists to document that preparation: paper reviews, implementation notes, and technical logs that reflect how I think about research problems. It is not a general portfolio; it is focused deliberately.

---

## Research Orientation

I am studying the intersection of three areas:

**Geometric vision and 3D scene representations.** I read deeply in 3D Gaussian Splatting, implicit neural representations, part-level 3D understanding, and optimization-based reconstruction. The question I keep returning to: what structural properties make a scene representation actionable for a robot, not just visually plausible?

**Teleoperation and robot–object interaction.** My interest here is practical: how does a system move from raw sensor input to a representation that supports contact-rich manipulation? I follow work on object-centric representations, demonstration reuse, and data-efficient learning for manipulation.

**Deployable robotics software.** My internship at ROBROS gave me direct experience with the infrastructure layer — real-time DDS tuning, heterogeneous network management, RealSense and stereo camera pipelines. I think about software systems as a prerequisite to research, not separate from it.
