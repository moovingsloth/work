Research Orientation

My reading and implementation work concentrates on three overlapping problem areas where geometric understanding, neural scene representations, and deployable robot systems converge.

Geometric Vision & 3D Scene Understanding

I study representations that preserve spatial structure and support downstream manipulation tasks: 3D Gaussian Splatting, part-level segmentation, object-centric scene graphs, and optimization-grounded reconstruction. The emphasis is on what the geometry affords physically, not just visually.

Teleoperation & Robot–Object Interaction

I trace the data pipeline from sensor capture through scene encoding to action generation. My background in real-time DDS communication and RealSense / stereo camera infrastructure grounds this in deployment constraints, not just algorithmic ideals.

Deployable Research Software

I have experience building and maintaining systems that run continuously: heterogeneous LAN/WiFi robotics networks, Kubernetes-based ML serving, and multimodal data pipelines. I approach implementation as a first-class research activity.
